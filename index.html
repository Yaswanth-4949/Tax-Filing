What taxes are deducted from my paycheck? +
H1B workers are subject to federal income tax, Social Security tax, and Medicare tax. State income tax may also apply.
Can I claim dependents on my tax return? +
Yes, if you meet the requirements for resident alien status and provide the necessary documentation for your dependents.
Am I eligible for tax credits like the Child Tax Credit or the Earned Income Tax Credit? +
As a resident alien, you may be eligible for these credits, provided you meet income and filing requirements.
What expenses can I deduct as an H1B worker? +
While the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act eliminated most employee business expense deductions, you can still deduct expenses related to moving (if applicable) or education if they qualify under current tax laws.
Do I need to file taxes as an F1 visa holder, even if I didn‚Äôt earn any income? +
Yes, all F1 visa holders must file Form 8843 annually, even if they have no income.
Am I considered a resident or non-resident alien for tax purposes? +
F1 visa holders are generally considered non-resident aliens for the first five calendar years of their stay in the U.S. under the substantial presence test.
What forms should I use to file my taxes? +
Non-resident aliens usually file Form 1040-NR, but if you qualify as a resident alien, you may need to file Form 1040.
Are scholarships or fellowships taxable? +
Scholarships and fellowships used for tuition, fees, and books are generally not taxable. However, funds used for living expenses, such as room and board, are taxable.
Can I claim any deductions or tax treaties as an F1 student? +
Depending on your country of origin, you may be eligible for benefits under a tax treaty between the U.S. and your home country. Common deductions include student loan interest and some education-related expenses.
